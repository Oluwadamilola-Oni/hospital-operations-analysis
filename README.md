# 🏥 Hospital Operations Analysis
SQL and Power BI analysis of hospital operations to uncover insights on patient flow, length of stay, and treatment costs.


## 🔍 Project Overview
This project analyzes hospital operations data to identify key factors affecting patient flow, length of stay, and treatment costs. The goal is to generate insights that can support operational efficiency and better resource allocation in healthcare settings.

---

## 🧰 Tools Used
- SQL (MySQL) – Data querying and analysis  
- Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard development  

---

## 📁 Dataset
Synthetic dataset representing hospital patient records, including:
- Department
- Admission type
- Admission and discharge dates
- Length of stay
- Treatment cost
- Bed occupancy

---

## 📊 Key Business Questions
- Which department has the longest patient stay?
- Which admission type leads to longer hospital stays?
- How do treatment costs vary across departments?
- Is there a relationship between length of stay and cost?
- Are there seasonal patterns in patient admissions?

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- Cardiology recorded the highest average length of stay, suggesting more complex cases or longer treatment pathways.

- Orthopedics had the highest treatment cost, indicating resource-intensive procedures such as surgeries.

- Short hospital stays (1–2 days) showed higher average costs, suggesting that intensive treatments may occur within short durations.

- Walk-in patients had the longest hospital stays, indicating possible delays in early intervention.

- Admissions peaked in May and were lowest in April, suggesting variations in hospital demand.

- Despite longer stays in cardiology, orthopedics incurred higher costs, showing that duration does not always directly translate to financial burden.

---

## 💡 Business Implications

- Hospitals should balance both cost and duration when allocating resources across departments.

- Encouraging early referrals may reduce prolonged hospital stays associated with walk-in patients.

- High-cost short stays should be further investigated to improve cost efficiency.

- Seasonal trends should be considered in staffing and capacity planning.
